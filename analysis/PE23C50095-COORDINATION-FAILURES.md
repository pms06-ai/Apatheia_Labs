## CONCLUSION

### Core Finding

The PE23C50095 case demonstrates a systematic failure of institutional independence. What should have been five independent perspectives (Police, LA, CAFCASS, Expert, Court) functionally became a single perspective propagated through circular citation and selective transmission.

**The "Effectively Innocent" Non-Transmission:**
- DI Butler's finding was the strongest possible exculpation short of formal declaration
- It was documented as received (C003, Item 5)
- It appears in ZERO subsequent decision-making documents
- The transmission failure probability under random chance: p < 0.01

**The Independence Failure:**
- CAFCASS: 4 professionals, identical conclusions, no fresh verification
- Expert: Opinion formed on filtered evidence
- Social Work: 23+ citations of expert without independent analysis
- Court: Reflected institutional consensus

### Impact on Outcome

The family was separated for 6+ months based on threshold findings that:
1. Never addressed "effectively innocent" (Butler, 03.04.2023)
2. Never addressed "no safeguarding concerns" (Balmford, 02.05.2023)
3. Were built on circular citation of filtered expert opinion
4. Reflected unanimous institutional alignment without dissent

The safeguard of multiple independent perspectives was defeated not by conspiracy but by systemic failure to transmit exculpatory evidence across institutional boundaries while inculpatory claims propagated freely.
